Starting your journey as a commercial pilot is an exciting yet challenging endeavor. Here's everything you need to know about navigating this rewarding career path in India.

## Understanding the Requirements

Before you begin your training, you need to meet certain eligibility criteria set by the Directorate General of Civil Aviation (DGCA). These include educational qualifications, medical standards, and age requirements.

The minimum educational requirement is 50% marks in Physics and Mathematics in your 12th standard or equivalent. You also need to obtain a Class 1 or Class 2 medical certificate from a DGCA-approved medical examiner.

## Choosing the Right Flight School

Selecting the appropriate flight school is crucial for your aviation career. Look for schools that are DGCA-approved and have a strong track record of student success.

Consider factors such as fleet quality, instructor experience, pass rates, and placement assistance. Vervve Academy offers comprehensive training programs designed to prepare you for the airline industry.

## The Training Timeline

The path to becoming a commercial pilot typically takes 18-24 months. This includes ground school training, simulator sessions, and actual flight hours.

You'll need to complete approximately 200 flight hours, including solo flights, cross-country flights, and night flying. Each phase builds upon the previous one, gradually preparing you for the responsibilities of a commercial pilot.

## Building Your Career

After obtaining your Commercial Pilot License (CPL), the next step is building your career. Many pilots start as first officers with regional carriers before progressing to major airlines.

Networking, maintaining your licenses, and continuously upgrading your skills are essential for long-term success in this competitive field.
